Abstract

This project explores the growing trend of vegan, vegetarian, and gluten-free dining in New York City, revealing how these options are distributed across the boroughs and influenced by cultural and demographic factors. Manhattan leads in availability, while Brooklyn and Queens show steady growth, and the Bronx and Staten Island present opportunities for expansion. Popular cuisines like Italian and Mexican dominate the vegan dining scene, reflecting the city’s rich cultural diversity.
Using interactive visualizations, we connected health-conscious dining trends with demographics and exercise habits over time. The insights highlight a shift toward healthier lifestyles and offer guidance for restaurateurs and policymakers to expand sustainable dining options across NYC.

Introduction

New York City, a culinary hub celebrated for its diversity, has embraced the rising demand for vegan, vegetarian, and gluten-free dining options. This project examines the landscape of plant-based and health-conscious restaurants across the city’s boroughs, exploring connections between cuisine types, demographic influences, and lifestyle trends. By analyzing data and leveraging interactive visualizations, we gain insights into how cultural preferences, population dynamics, and health awareness shape the availability of these dining options.
With Manhattan at the forefront, followed by Brooklyn and Queens, the study sheds light on borough-specific trends and highlights potential growth areas in the Bronx and Staten Island. This analysis serves not only to understand current patterns but also to guide future expansion for sustainable dining, reflecting broader shifts toward healthier, more inclusive food choices.

What We Learned...

Through this project, we focused on crafting clear and meaningful visualizations that effectively communicate the data's story. Here's how we achieved this:

  • Simplified Visuals: I applied the data-ink ratio concept by keeping charts clean and focusing on the essential information, avoiding unnecessary design clutter.
  • Intuitive Grouping: Gestalt principles like proximity and similarity helped me create visual groupings, such as linking demographics and boroughs in the sunburst chart.
  • Accessible Colors: I used colorblind-friendly palettes to ensure the visualizations were easy to interpret for all viewers.
  • Clear and Honest Design: I followed visualization maxims to compare data meaningfully, keep visuals simple, and highlight key insights without distorting the message.
  • Thoughtful Encodings: I chose visual elements like bar lengths for precise comparisons and areas or angles for broader overviews, ensuring the data matched the most intuitive representation.
