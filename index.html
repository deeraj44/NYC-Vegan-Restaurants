Abstract

This project investigates the evolving landscape of vegan, vegetarian, and gluten-free dining in New York City by analyzing restaurant data alongside demographic, economic, lifestyle and cultural trends. Using interactive data visualization techniques to study and uncover key insights about the distribution of health-conscious dining options across NYC's boroughs. Manhattan emerges as a hotspot for vegan dining, with Brooklyn and Queens showing promising growth. In contrast, the Bronx and Staten Island reveal gaps in the availability of these dining options, pointing to opportunities for expansion. Popular cuisines such as Italian, Mexican, and American dominate the vegan dining scene, reflecting diverse cultural preferences and culinary influences. Additionally, this analysis examines correlations between demographic factors, such as ethnicity and population size, and the prevalence of vegan restaurants. The inclusion of exercise trends over two decades further contextualizes the rise of health-conscious dining as part of broader lifestyle shifts. Through dynamic visualizations, including heatmaps, treemaps, and trend lines, this study provides actionable insights for policymakers, restaurateurs, and urban planners to foster inclusive and sustainable dining ecosystems. These findings underscore the importance of data-driven decision-making in shaping the future of NYC's food culture.

Introduction

New York City, a culinary hub celebrated for its diversity, has embraced the rising demand for vegan, vegetarian, and gluten-free dining options. This project examines the landscape of plant-based and health-conscious restaurants across the city’s boroughs, exploring connections between cuisine types, demographic influences, and lifestyle trends. By analyzing data and leveraging interactive visualizations, we gain insights into how cultural preferences, population dynamics, and health awareness shape the availability of these dining options.
With Manhattan at the forefront, followed by Brooklyn and Queens, the study sheds light on borough-specific trends and highlights potential growth areas in the Bronx and Staten Island. This analysis serves not only to understand current patterns but also to guide future expansion for sustainable dining, reflecting broader shifts toward healthier, more inclusive food choices.

Motivation

The motivation for this project stems from the growing popularity of vegan and vegetarian lifestyles and their influence on the restaurant industry, particularly in a diverse and bustling hub like New York City. With increasing awareness around health, sustainability, and ethical eating, plant-based dining has become a significant trend. This project sought to analyze the distribution and dynamics of vegan and vegetarian restaurants across NYC's boroughs, examining how they cater to both local residents and the thriving tourism industry. By uncovering patterns shaped by demographics and regional preferences, the project aims to provide actionable insights for restaurant owners and city planners. These insights can help restaurant owners align their offerings with customer demand and assist policymakers in promoting plant-based options in key tourist and residential areas. Ultimately, this work highlights how vegan and vegetarian dining can enrich NYC's culinary landscape while catering to the needs of both its residents and millions of visitors.

What We Learned...

Through this project, our team focused on crafting clear and meaningful visualizations that effectively communicate the data's story. Here's how we collectively achieved this:

  • Simplified Visuals: We applied the data-ink ratio concept by keeping charts clean and focusing on the essential information, avoiding unnecessary design clutter.
  • Intuitive Grouping: Using Gestalt principles like proximity and similarity, we created visual groupings, such as linking demographics and boroughs in the sunburst chart, to enhance interpretability.
  • Accessible Colors: We ensured the visualizations were inclusive by utilizing colorblind-friendly palettes, making them easy to interpret for all viewers.
  • Clear and Honest Design: We adhered to visualization maxims to compare data meaningfully, keep visuals simple, and highlight key insights without distorting the message.
  • Thoughtful Encodings: Our team carefully selected visual elements, such as bar lengths for precise comparisons and areas or angles for broader overviews, ensuring the data was represented in the most intuitive and impactful way.

Methodology

Data Scraping: Scraped current restaurants and gym data using yelp API
Integration: Merged demographic data with restaurant locations
Data Cleaning: Removed duplicates from restaurant datasets
Visualization: Created interactive charts using Python libraries
Standardization: Normalized borough names and cuisine
Analysis: Generated insights from borough-wise distribution
Visualization Techniques: Choropleth map, Treemap, Charts
Presentation: Developed clear visual hierarchy for data story
